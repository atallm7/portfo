Project 1

Project Design 1 aimed to create a wind blade for support in Guatemala, emphasizing the importance of considering the country's weather conditions and limited maintenance resources. The key takeaway underscores the necessity of designing wind turbines with a focus on lifetime and low maintenance costs. Factors such as selecting durable materials, optimizing blade design for maximum wind speed capture, and implementing efficient energy storage systems were identified as critical elements for ensuring a consistent electricity supply. The recommendation for future engineering initiatives is to prioritize advancements in blade materials and designs that enhance efficiency and durability, aligning with the unique challenges posed by Guatemala's environment.

Project 2

Project 2 team MON-27 collaborated with The Canadian Space Agency on a project to robotically sterilize surgical tools for space missions. The modeling sub-team designed a specialized container, shaped for a retractor, with features such as autoclave-accessible holes, a closing lid, and interior extrusions for secure transportation. Through iterative design, the final container met weight and print time constraints, ensuring overall sterility. The coding sub-team developed an algorithm for a Q arm, employing coordinates and sensors for precise pickup and drop-off at autoclave locations. The breakthrough solution promises efficiency and automation, with potential applications extending beyond space missions.
